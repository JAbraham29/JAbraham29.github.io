NBA MVP 2016-2017

Intro - Player Value - Rankings - Further Exploration - Links

These are my findings based on the three statistics that I looked at (WS, PER, and VORP). My findings were determined in my midterm project before Russell Westbrook was announced to be the winner about a month ago. The Most Valuable Player award is subjective, since it is decided on by a panel of media personnel with different criteria for evaluation. You can evaluate other statistics for the players that had a PER over 25, WS over 10 and VORP over 5 in the final scene on the next page and come to your own conclusion of who rightly deserved the MVP award of the 2016-2017 NBA season.

For this project, I used a Martini Glass narrative visualization structure. I begin with author-driven content for the first 3 scenes (specifically including only statistics that I thought best determined which player was the most valuable to his team) and end with reader-driven content by allowing the reader to compare the top performers this year based on other metrics that were in the dataset.

Scenes: There are a total of 4 scenes in this narrative visualization, which include a scatterplot, bar charts, and tables. We begin by seeing an overview of the data in a scatterplot for what I think are 2 of the best metrics for evaluating overall performance, Win Shares and Player Efficiency Rating.

Annotations: For this visualization, the annotation is the legend that color codes the players by position. Looking at the top right portion of the plot, it appears that many of the top players play the Small Forward position. The size of the points is associated with how many minutes that player played to negate the situations where a player performed extremely well in limited minutes. Parameters: The axes change in this visualization since the plot is draggable and zoomable.

Triggers: Use the mouse wheel to zoom or click and drag the mouse to view other portions of the graph. Also, if you hover over each point, a tooltip will appear providing more information.
